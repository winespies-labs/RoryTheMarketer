# Wine Spies: Operative Subtypes

Customers are called **Operatives**. They are not casual wine browsers. Every subtype below is asking the same core question: *"Is this intelligently priced relative to quality and market?"* — not just "will I like this?"

Messaging that fails to answer that question, for the right subtype, will underperform.

---

## 1. The Value Analyst

**Core motivation:** Logic-first buyer. Wants quantifiable proof the wine is priced below market.

**Primary question:** "Can I prove this is a steal?"

**Decision filters:**
- Winery release price
- Wine-Searcher comparison
- % below retail
- Comparable AVA pricing

**What they need in copy:**
- Concrete price comparison (not just the % off)
- Clear market positioning
- Proof, not adjectives

**Lines that land:**
- "Priced 40% below winery direct."
- "Lowest listed price in the country."
- "Runs with [$150 bottle] — at $49."

**Overlaps with:** Insider Hunter (both respond to name drops and credibility anchors)

**Example operatives:** Geoff Gibson, Snowy Bramble, Anton
**Example wines:** Firefly 2008 Cabernet, Gandona Cabernet, Clos des Papes

---

## 2. The Insider Hunter

**Core motivation:** Access. Wines that feel scarce, allocated, or normally out of reach.

**Primary question:** "Is this scarce, allocated, or under-the-radar?"

**Decision filters:**
- Case production numbers
- Allocation / mailing list status
- Vineyard prestige
- Distribution limitations

**What they need in copy:**
- Production numbers (specific is better)
- Allocation or mailing list callout
- Vineyard sourcing details
- Scarcity with credibility — not manufactured urgency

**Lines that land:**
- "Only 72 cases released outside the mailing list."
- "From fruit typically reserved for $100+ bottlings."
- "Rarely seen outside California — first time on Wine Spies."

**Example operatives:** Som Chivukula, Bob Martel, Kimberly Barnish
**Example producers:** Gary Farrell, Lerner, Ridge, DANA Estates

---

## 3. The Collector-Curious

**Core motivation:** Buy wines with structure and aging potential — without overpaying for trophy labels.

**Primary question:** "Is this built properly for cellaring?"

**Decision filters:**
- Vintage quality
- Tannin structure
- Oak regimen
- Balance and acidity
- Cellar window

**What they need in copy:**
- Structural detail (oak regimen, tannin profile)
- Drinking window
- Vintage context
- Producer credibility that earns trust

**Lines that land:**
- "22 months in French oak. Drinking window: 2026–2038."
- "Built for the cellar — but drinks beautifully now."
- "The kind of structure that demands patience and rewards it."

**Overlaps with:** Confident Experimenter (both are branching into new areas)

**Example operatives:** Wendy Charlier, Leonie Fedel
**Example wines:** Saini red blend, Karl Erbes Riesling, Rhônes, Bordeaux, imports

---

## 4. The Confident Experimenter

**Core motivation:** Discover intelligently. Boutique producers, emerging regions — but only when credibility is earned.

**Primary question:** "Is this a producer or region worth knowing?"

**Decision filters:**
- Boutique / small production
- Emerging or underrepresented regions
- Winemaker pedigree
- Sub-AVA specificity

**What they need in copy:**
- Context for WHY this producer matters
- Subregional specificity (not just "Sonoma" — Petaluma Gap, Ribbon Ridge, etc.)
- Case production numbers
- Credibility cues that establish this isn't random

**Lines that land:**
- "Family-run Oakville estate producing under 2,000 cases annually."
- "Santa Lucia Highlands producer rarely seen outside California."
- "If you know Bedrock, you understand why this matters."

**Buying behavior:** Often buys 1–2 bottles. Wide variety of styles. High trust in Wine Spies' judgment.

**Example operatives:** Mark Stephens, Cathleen Markham
**Example wines:** Dolcetto, Oomrang, Aligote, Grenache, Mythical Albariño

---

## Applying Personas to Copy Tasks

When generating any copy asset, identify the primary target operative and weight the messaging accordingly:

| If targeting... | Lead with... | Proof point to include |
|----------------|--------------|----------------------|
| Value Analyst | Price vs. market | Wine-Searcher comp, winery direct price |
| Insider Hunter | Scarcity + access | Case production, allocation status |
| Collector-Curious | Structure + cellar potential | Oak regimen, drinking window, vintage |
| Confident Experimenter | Why this producer matters | Sub-AVA, winemaker pedigree, case count |

Most offers will appeal to 2 subtypes. Pick the primary, let the secondary show up in the supporting detail.
