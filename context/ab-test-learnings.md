# Wine Spies Copy: A/B Test Learnings

This file contains empirical data from controlled A/B tests on Wine Spies offer copy.
These are not opinions. These are results. Weight them heavily.

---

## Summary Stats (15 Tests)

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Tests where variation beat control (add to cart) | 11 of 15 (73%) |
| Average lift in add-to-cart when variation won | +36.8% |
| Best single campaign lift (add to cart) | +170% (Dashe Bedrock) |
| Average drop when variation lost | -3% (negligible) |
| Best revenue lift | +107% (Ten Acre Chardonnay) |
| Winning copy avg word count | ~200 words |
| Losing copy avg word count | ~350–400 words |
| Winning copy avg Flesch-Kincaid | 8.2 |
| Losing copy avg Flesch-Kincaid | 10.6 |

**The asymmetry matters:** When the shorter, punchier copy won, it won big. When it lost, it barely lost. The risk/reward strongly favors the direct style.

---

## What the Winning Copy Did

### 1. Led with the deal or a bold hook
Every winning variation started with something that grabbed attention — a comparison, a bold claim, or the price drop front-loaded.

### 2. Used analogies instead of wine descriptions
> "The Corvette C8 of Chardonnay"  
> "Running low and moving fast like a dachshund race"  
> "Faster than a fresh drop of toilet paper at Costco"

### 3. Used juicy, relatable flavor descriptors — not traditional tasting notes
> ❌ "Notes of blackberry macerated in cloves with toasted oak"  
> ✅ "Fresh baked cherry pie, cocoa dusting, and a pepper kick"

### 4. Made scarcity concrete and specific
> "Only 20 barrels exist. Two came to the US."  
> "We bought everything we could. Was it enough? Probably not."

### 5. Ended with a punchy close
Not "we hope you enjoy this wine." More like:
> "Why are you still reading this??"  
> "Grab some before it's gone!"  
> "Press pause on whatever you're doing and throw some in your cart."

## Winning Copy Examples (Reference These)

### L'Alberello (32% lift in cart)
- **Hook:** Superman vs Batman analogy leading into a Super Tuscan throwdown
- **Key moves:** Named Sassicaia as neighbor to anchor value, made scarcity visceral ("barely enough to warrant an offer"), ended with urgency
- **Word count:** 213 | **FK:** 7.4

### Sullivan Cabernet (52% lift in cart)
- **Hook:** "Here's one for those who love big, rich, sultry Cabernet" — direct address
- **Key moves:** Named elite neighbors (Caymus, Staglin, Quintessa), told the bankruptcy story as VALUE context, used short punchy critic quotes
- **Word count:** 205 | **FK:** 7.4

### Ten Acre Chardonnay (28% lift cart, 107% revenue lift)
- **Hook:** "Corvette C8 of Chardonnay — runs with the big boys at a fraction of the price"
- **Key moves:** Named Aubert as the benchmark producer (same fruit source), used Vivino quote conversationally, closed with "At this price? Unbeatable."
- **Word count:** 195 | **FK:** 8.0

### Dashe Bedrock Zinfandel (170% lift in cart, 88% lift thanks page, 47% revenue lift)
- **Hook:** "Another colossal triumph — Bedrock, baby!!"
- **Key moves:** Brief vineyard history (planted 1854, Sherman/Hooker), winemaker pedigree (Lafite, Cloudy Bay, Ridge), ended with "Why are you still reading this??"
- **Word count:** 210 | **FK:** 9.6

### Beekeeper Zinfandel (10% lift in cart)
- **Hook:** Named the competition (Biale, Bedrock, Carlisle, Turley) then said "But this Beekeeper? WHOA."
- **Key moves:** Let the wine score speak (94/94/97+), flavor notes as emotional experience not clinical description, closed with scarcity
- **Word count:** 160 | **FK:** 8.0

## What the Losing Copy Did

1. **Started with region/vintage history** before getting to the wine or deal
2. **Used formal tasting note structure** (color → nose → palate → finish → food pairing)
3. **Buried the price or discount** instead of leading with it
4. **Quoted critics at length** verbatim instead of summarizing
5. **Ended with food pairing suggestions** — which killed momentum
6. **Higher reading level** — required more cognitive effort

---

## Rules Derived from Data

1. **Under 220 words.** Aim for 160–210.
2. **FK grade 5–7 target.** Hard ceiling of 8.
3. **Lead with hook, not history.**
4. **One or two critic scores, embedded naturally** — don't list multiple publications formally.
5. **Make scarcity specific** — numbers, barrels, cases when available.
6. **End with urgency** — close the sale, don't summarize the wine.
7. **Use analogies** for flavor, for value, for excitement — they outperform clinical descriptors.
