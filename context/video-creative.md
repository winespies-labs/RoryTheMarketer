This file is the primary reference for all video briefs. Load alongside `voice-guidelines.md` for any video task.

## Shot Ideas (UGC / Creator Briefs)

Use these as direction for creators or as scene suggestions in briefs:

- Unboxing a recent Locker delivery
- Showcasing one or two wines you recently had or are excited to open
- Explaining what you look for when buying from Wine Spies (everyday values vs. rare/collectible)
- Talking to the camera like you're telling a friend — "what would you say to get them to check it out?"
- Opening the wine fridge and pulling out a bottle

## Proven Hook Lines

These are plug-and-play openers. Use verbatim or riff on them:

**Deal / Value Hooks**
- "Want a $100 Napa Cab for $30? Then stop scrolling"
- "Here's how I built a wine collection without going broke"
- "How I [do X] while on a budget"
- "Don't buy that, buy this instead!"
- "Stop scrolling if you want the best deals on great wine"

**Discovery / FOMO Hooks**
- "Three wines that absolutely floored me"
- "I never would have found this wine if it weren't for Wine Spies"
- "I used to buy the same wines from the grocery store. Now I can't imagine doing that."
- "Things I Wish I Knew About Sooner"
- "If you're not doing X, you're missing out"

**Lifestyle / Relatable Hooks**
- "Just got my delivery from Wine Spies"
- "Getting my daily Wine Spies email is like Christmas morning…"
- "One thing I use every day without fail is…"
- "If you're like me and do X, then this one's for you"
- "I Bought This For My [person] For [occasion]"

**Curiosity / Intrigue Hooks**
- "Here's my secret to finding incredible wines at unbelievable prices"
- "TikTok made me buy this"
- "What I ordered vs. what I got" *(works well with a reveal)*
- "My top shopping find I now can't live without"

## Hook Frameworks

Use these to build NEW hooks from scratch:

### The Contrarian
Challenge a common belief.
> "Most people think you need to spend $80 on a wine to get something great. You don't."

### The Teacher
Lead with a category, offer a recommendation.
> "If you like Pinot Noir, you need to try this."
> "If you love Napa Cab but hate Napa Cab prices, keep watching."

### The Investigator
Reveal a secret or expose something. Creates contrast between "before you knew" and "now you know."
> "You don't need to spend $100 on a Napa Cab. Here's why."
> "Most people overpay for wine. Here's what they're missing."

### The Magician
Visually-led. Something unexpected happens in the first frame. Movement, reveal, or visual contrast grabs attention before words.
> Opens with a wine fridge stuffed with bottles → "All from Wine Spies."
> Dramatic bottle pour → price tag reveal.
> Uses "Check this out…" as a lead-in before a quick scene cut.

### The Experimenter
Personal story angle. "I tried it, here's what happened."
> "I switched from buying wine at the grocery store to Wine Spies. Here's what changed."

### The Fortune Teller
Make a bold prediction or promise.
> "By the end of this video, you're going to want to check your inbox."

## Approved Script Examples

These are tested, on-brand scripts. Use as templates or lift language directly.

---

### Script Option 1: The Secret
**Angle:** Insider tip, personal discovery
**Length:** ~30s
**Best for:** Acquisition, cold audience

> Here's my secret to finding incredible wines at unbelievable prices. It's called Wine Spies. Every day they drop a new deal, and if I see something I like, I buy it. You can pick one or two bottles — and the best part is you can build a case that ships free when it's full. But a lot of the wines sell out quickly. Which is sometimes a blessing in disguise, because honestly, I'm running out of space to store all these wines.

**Notes:** Conversational, first-person, ends with a relatable/self-deprecating closer. Don't over-produce this one — it works better feeling natural.

---

### Script Option 2: The Insider
**Angle:** "Best deals in the world" + Locker explainer
**Length:** ~45s
**Best for:** Acquisition, slightly warmer audience

> If you want to find the best deals on incredible wines, you need to know about Wine Spies. Every day they offer one insanely good deal at the lowest price in the world. I just check my email to see the daily offer, and if it sounds good, I buy it. The best part is you can build a case — one or two bottles at a time — and ship it for free anytime you're ready. I've found some shockingly good deals on wines I never thought I'd see discounted. And discovered plenty of small producers. The customer service is unmatched. The only problem? I have no more space to store all the wine I bought.

**Notes:** Covers the full value prop — daily deal, lowest price, Locker, discovery, service. Good all-in-one script for a broad audience.

---

### Script Option 3: The Wine Collector
**Angle:** Social proof via collection size
**Length:** ~30–45s
**Best for:** Aspirational/collector audience, organic social

> Here's my collection of [100 / 200 / 300+] bottles. And I think 80% of them came from Wine Spies. Let me show you a few favorites…
> *[Multiple cuts showing different bottles, labels, vintages]*

**Notes:** Works best with a real collection on camera. The visual does the selling — script is just a frame. Creator should pick 3–5 bottles to highlight with brief commentary on each ("This one I got for $40, it's a 95-point Sonoma Pinot. Sold out in a few hours.").

---

### Script Option 4: The Locker Explainer
**Angle:** Feature education, removes objection
**Length:** ~30–45s
**Best for:** Retargeting, mid-funnel, Locker acquisition

> Why is Wine Spies so popular? Well, first — they get deals that nobody else does. Like this [wine], this [wine], and this [wine]. But my favorite part is the Locker. I hate having to buy 3 or 6 bottles at a time just to get free shipping from other places. What if I don't love the wine? Or I don't want to be home multiple days in a row signing for packages?

> With Lockers I can minimize deliveries and maximize variety. And I don't pay for shipping. You fill your Locker a bottle or two at a time, build up a case of 12, and ship it free whenever you're ready.

**Notes:** This script is the best explainer for the Locker feature. Lead with the deals to hook attention, then pivot to Locker as "the best part." The objection being overcome is real and relatable — don't soften it.

## UGC Creator Direction Notes

When briefing creators, include:
- **Tone:** Real, casual, like telling a friend. Not scripted-feeling.
- **Setting:** Home, wine fridge, kitchen, dining table — authentic environments
- **Wardrobe:** Relaxed. No brand guidelines to follow — just be themselves.
- **Deliverables:** Raw footage preferred. Minimum: 1 hero cut + 3 hook variants.
- **Don't:** Use wine influencer clichés (swirling glass into camera, overly dramatic pours, fake reactions)
- **Do:** React genuinely. The self-deprecating "now I have no room for more wine" closer works every time.
