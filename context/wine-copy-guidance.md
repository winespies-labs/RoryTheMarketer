# Wine Copy Guidance

## Introduction / Lede Fundamentals

These are all elements that should be in the first few sentences of every writeup. Not every one, but use where/when relevant.

### Slippery Slope

The first sentence of every writeup should be a slippery slope. The ONLY goal of this sentence is to get them to read the next one.

Use a few consistent strategies to set the hook.

### Hooks

- **Promise**
- **Proof**
- **Empathy**
- **Contrarian**
- **Statistic**
- **Massive mistake**
- **Story**
- **Question**

### Price Anchoring

> "You'd pay $175 for this Beckstoffer Cab anywhere else, but today, while it lasts it's $49."

> "Comparable wines from this vineyard include X, X, and X which sell for $299, $187, and $150 respectively."

### Scarcity

> "Only 100 cases were made and we got but a fraction of that."

> "Knowing how quickly Ops buy up anything made by Thomas Rivers Brown, we're already lining up a backup for when this sells out"

### Tasting Notes

"Sell the sizzle, not the steak" — pull quotes from reviews or tasting notes up top.

### Discounts

Use the larger number. For example if the discount is $99 that's better than 45% off. But either way, always lean into either one somewhere near the top/lead of the writeup.

### Social Proof

Usually includes either a critic's score, or something gleaned from Cellartracker/Vivino or the Facebook group.

> "Pete Maren has been begging us nonstop in the chat for this wine"

> "Here's what one Op had to say about the previous vintage of this wine — 'Wow I nearly shit my pants when I tasted this! SO GOOD'"

### Points / Scores

> "The Fresno State Fair awarded this 102 points"

> "97 POINTS from Jeb Dunnuck!"

### Relatability, Humor, and Personality

> "When Agent Noir went to Burgundy for the first time this was the wine that rewired circuits in his brain."

> "Did you see that home run that Barry Bonds hit last night? We're crushing it."

## General Style Guide

### Readability

Content should be at a **5–6th grade level** reading comprehension. The Flesch-Kincaid scale is the basis. Use [Hemingway App](https://hemingwayapp.com/readability-checker) to test.

If you can use fewer words to get a point across, do it. Use fewer words to make your point.

### Transitional Phrases

Use [SmartBlogger's transitional phrases](https://smartblogger.com/transitional-phrases/) as reference:

- "Mind Reader" transition phrases
- "Can't Miss This" transition words
- "Important Insight" transition phrases
- "There's a Catch" transition words
- "Big Answer" transition phrases
- "But Wait, There's More" transition words
- "Exemplary Example" transition phrases
- "Lifting the Veil" transition words
- "How To" transition phrases
- "Stay with Me" transition words
- "Curious Question" transition phrases
- "Rhetorical Question" transition words
- "Guess What Happened" transition phrases

### Active Voice

Active voice always comes across more direct and links the noun with the verb better. Lawyers use passive voice to remove their defendant from the situation.

> **Passive:** "This wine was awarded 97 points by Jeb Dunnuck"
>
> **Active:** "Jebby D blessed this with 97 points."

### Short Paragraphs and 1–2 Sentence Breaks

Keep writeups broken up — no more than 2–3 sentences in a paragraph. The visual should look like a lightning bolt:

> Short sentence.
>
> You want this wine because of this.
>
> Boom.

### Exaggeration / Hype

> "Forget about the Hadron Super Collider, because we discovered something that puts the Higgs Boson particle to shame. Obsessed with finding dark matter? Look no further because Collide Cabernet Sauvignon is here. This is a black hole deep and concentrated wine that blows other $100 Napa Cabernets out of this universe."

> "Like 'the catch' by Joe Montana to Dwight Clark or 'The shot' by Jordan to beat the Cavs in the last second, having this wine here today is a 'see it to believe it' vinous miracle of the highest order."

### Pop Culture x Nostalgia x Framing Emotion

> "Remember that Price is Right game, the cliffhanger one? Where you guess the price of something, and for every dollar you're off the little yodely hiker guy chugs along until he plummets off the edge?"

## Working Examples of Revisions

### Kosta Convene

**Original:**

> Will it last the day? Probably not…. as Wine Spectator says, "Welcome Back, Kosta!"
>
> You may know that prices have soared well above $100 for Kosta Browne over the years. So, finding one of those names attached to a Pinot with a huge break is a dream come true for the fans.
>
> As Robb Report wrote, "the king of Sonoma Pinot Noir has created another outstanding winery. With his recently launched Convene by Dan Kosta, he has shown that lightning can strike twice in the same place."
>
> And it wasn't long ago that Wine Spectator raved "Vintner Dan Kosta is back, albeit he never really left. A founder of California Pinot Noir powerhouse Kosta Browne, Dan has launched a new Sonoma Chardonnay and Pinot Noir project called Convene. The name translates on different levels — it refers to Kosta being back in the wine game and to woof choice, Shane Finely."

**Revised:**

> Will a 94 POINT Kosta Pinot last the day?
>
> Probably not.
>
> "Welcome back, Kosta" said Wine Spectator.
>
> Diehard followers of Kosta Browne know one thing. Prices have soared over the years. Today you'll pay well over $100 for a bottle.
>
> So finding this?
>
> A literal dream come true for any of those devotees.
