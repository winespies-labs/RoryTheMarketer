# Wine Spies Operative Sub Types

## 1️⃣ The Value Analyst

The Value Analyst buys with logic first, emotion second. They want quantifiable proof that a wine is priced below market relative to its AVA, producer tier, and winery direct pricing. Discounts alone don’t impress them. The full context does. They respond to clear price comparisons, under-market positioning, and data-backed validation. They overlap with the “Insider Hunter” below as often they’re buying wines with big names/winemakers or some other “name drop” or clout grabbing type of angle.

Example Operatives: Geoff Gibson, Snowy Bramble, Anton

Examples wines: Firefly 2008 Cabernet, Gandona Cabernet, Clos des Papes

## 2️⃣ The Insider Hunter

The Insider Hunter is motivated by access. They want wines that feel scarce, allocated, or normally unavailable to the general public. Wines they’re shocked to see on the site. Production numbers, mailing list references, vineyard prestige, and distribution limitations matter more than discount percentages. They respond to credible scarcity and insider positioning.

Example Operatives: Som Chivukula, Bob Martel, Kimberly Barnish

Example producers: Gary Farrell, Lerner, Ridge, DANA Estates

## 3️⃣ The Collector-Curious

The Collector-Curious wants wines with structure and aging potential but doesn’t want to overpay for trophy labels. They care about vintage quality, oak regimen, tannin structure, and cellar window. They respond to technical detail and cues that the wine is built properly — not just flashy or from a big name. They’re trying to build a small collection and branching into different areas to understand new wines they might enjoy. This segment overlaps with the “Experimenter” below.

Example Operatives: Wendy Charlier, Leonie Fedel

Examples wines: Saini red blend, Karl Erbes Riesling, Rhones and Bordeaux and other imports

## 4️⃣ The Confident Experimenter

The Confident Experimenter enjoys discovering boutique producers and emerging regions — but only when credibility is established. They want context for why a producer or vineyard matters. They respond to sub-AVA specificity, winemaker pedigree, and small production details. They often buy one or two bottles at a time, have a huge variety of styles, and are willing to try just about anything because they trust our judgement. 

Example Operatives: Mark Stephens, Cathleen Markham

Example wines: Dolcetto, Oomrang, Aligote, Grenache, Mythical Albarino

## 1️⃣ The Value Analyst

**Core Motivation:**

Make objectively smart purchases.

**Primary Question:**

“Is this under market value relative to comparable bottles?”

**Decision Filter:**

- Winery release price
- Wine-Searcher comparison
- % below retail
- Comparable AVA pricing

**What They Need in Messaging:**

- Concrete price comparison
- Clear market positioning
- Quantified savings
- Proof, not adjectives

**They respond to:**

“Priced 40% below winery direct.”

“Lowest listed price in the country.”

---

## 2️⃣ The Insider Hunter

**Core Motivation:**

Access what most people can’t get.

**Primary Question:**

“Is this scarce, allocated, or under-the-radar?”

**Decision Filter:**

- Case production numbers
- Allocation status
- Vineyard prestige
- Distribution limitations

**What They Need in Messaging:**

- Production numbers
- Allocation specifics
- Vineyard sourcing details
- Scarcity with credibility

**They respond to:**

“Only 72 cases released outside the mailing list.”

“From fruit typically reserved for $100+ bottlings.”

---

## 3️⃣ The Collector-Curious

**Core Motivation:**

Buy wines with structure and aging potential — without overpaying.

**Primary Question:**

“Is this built properly for cellaring?”

**Decision Filter:**

- Vintage quality
- Tannin structure
- Oak regimen
- Balance and acidity

**What They Need in Messaging:**

- Structural detail
- Cellar window
- Vintage nuance
- Producer credibility

**They respond to:**

“22 months in French oak.”

“Drinking window: 2026–2038.”

---

## 4️⃣ The Confident Experimenter

**Core Motivation:**

Discover intelligently.

**Primary Question:**

“Is this a producer or region worth knowing?”

**Decision Filter:**

- Boutique producers
- Emerging regions
- Winemaker pedigree
- Sub-AVA detail

**What They Need in Messaging:**

- Context about why this producer matters
- Subregional specificity
- Case production
- Credibility cues

**They respond to:**

“Family-run Oakville estate producing under 2,000 cases annually.”

“Santa Lucia Highlands producer rarely seen outside California.”

---

# The Unifying Trait of All Operatives

They are not asking:

“Will I like this?”

They are asking:

“Is this intelligently priced relative to quality and market?”
